## Findings

- Thresh: the clean `origin/main` actor already uses the LoL-style green soul face, lantern, chain, and hook silhouette. The older black-hood screenshot came from an out-of-date dirty/runtime folder and should be corrected by syncing the merged mod folder. The 2026-06-14 rebuild replaces Q Death Sentence, W Dark Passage, E Flay, and R The Box runtime sheets from local imagegen sources. W now shows an actual grounded lantern and shield ring, E uses a broad ground chain-scythe push with positive Knockback, and Q remains a hook/chain projectile instead of a generic green blob.
- Thresh R follow-up: The previous `ParabolicProjectile` anchor route could hide The Box's real damage/control from AI scoring and leave the prison VFX buried in end effects. The accepted 2026-06-14 pass exposes a direct top-level `RangeEffect` around Thresh for the AI-readable hit, spawns the generated high-z The Box ViewEffect immediately, and keeps the long-lived `RangePeriodProjectile` field for 540 ticks. The committed R sheet is non-linear bitmap prison art, not a code-generated pentagon.
- Blitzcrank: The old W/E path still described and applied a Mana Barrier-style shield, Q could read as a weak stop instead of a hard pull, and Static Field's low-z pulse/field could disappear under combat clutter. The 2026-06-14 rebuild removes the shield fallback, makes Q a fast non-piercing Rocket Grab that stuns and MoveTo-pulls the target back to a visible in-front stop, turns W into a strong move-speed/attack-speed Overdrive with post-buff fatigue, primes E as a next-hit Power Fist with Airborne and movement-skill lockout, and exposes R as a direct large AoE BlockSkill/silence pulse plus a generated lingering terrain field. CI and local runtime checks now block regressions to shield fallback, low-z Static Field, teleport/knockback hook logic, body-overlap pull endpoints, or line-art VFX.
- Blitzcrank actor follow-up: The skill pass left the robot's original body in place, but the legs were too dark and tiny in draft/card scaling. The actor follow-up keeps the approved torso, head, fists, frame count, and native `57x54` cells, then blends in image-generated lower-body/foot pixels only in the lower-center region of each upright frame. CI now requires readable lower-body and foot pixel ratios across idle, run, attack, skill, skill2, ult, and hit frames, so the model cannot regress to a torso-only card silhouette.
- Blitzcrank E follow-up: The image-generated Power Fist ready buff was authored at `80x72` with a visible fist wider than the actor body, so the long-lived buff made E activation look like the robot model deformed into a giant fist. The 2026-06-14 follow-up repacks that same generated texture into `48x48` compact frames with a max `46x32` visible bbox, leaving the larger `power_fist_impact` on the target-hit branch only. CI and local runtime checks now block actor-following Power Fist ready frames from becoming body-covering again.
- Blitzcrank compact portrait follow-up: After the actor and E fixes, the old `face x=-10,y=-8` camera still cropped compact side-list and 32px scoreboard avatars too low, emphasizing torso/shoulder armor instead of the head. The accepted 2026-06-14 face camera is `x=-8,y=0`; CI now checks both 40px side-list and 32px scoreboard cyan-core placement so the portrait cannot drift back to a torso crop.
- Darius: the first rebuilt ultimate still read too much like a target blood burst in live play, and the 2026-06-12 follow-up still did not sell the LoL-style skyfall execution strongly enough. The 2026-06-13 rebuild uses imagegen contact sheets chroma-keyed into transparent cast and hit sheets: the cast frames show Darius himself descending, landing, and chopping with the axe, while the hit frames hold the red-black impact, steel shards, cracks, and particle aftermath. CI now blocks the old effect-only/coded-line interpretation by requiring body pixels, dark armor, axe pixels, broad landing frames, and generated color detail.
- Kayn: The earlier R put its actual `RushMoveToBack` damage branch behind a 36-tick `Delayed` node and rendered the entry marker as a low-z caster-following effect while the caster was invisible, which made AI release scoring and live visibility unreliable. The 2026-06-14 follow-up exposes a direct top-level R rush/attack branch, keeps the target entry and exit VFX at high z, and replaces Q/W/R with image-generated Reaping Slash, Blade's Reach, red-eye entry mark, and darkin rupture sheets. W is now a long straight ground strike instead of another Q-like crescent.
- Kayn compact portrait follow-up: The previous `face x=-18,y=0` camera used the wrong horizontal side for the in-game compact crop, so the side-list and 32px scoreboard could show only the red-blue scythe edge. The accepted 2026-06-14 camera is `x=20,y=0`; CI now models Kayn's compact crop in the same direction as the game and checks both 40px side-list and 32px scoreboard face/hair placement.
- Kayn R visibility follow-up: The previous R still put `test_mod_kayn_r_entry` on a following view-effect row and fired `test_mod_kayn_r_exit` as a top-level cast-start effect. In live play that could collapse to sound-only feedback because the caster immediately enters the hidden/stasis portion and the release burst is not tied to the rush hit. The accepted 2026-06-14 data chain keeps the entry as a high-z non-following target-ground mark and spawns the exit ViewEffect inside the direct `RushMoveToBack` release branch with the hit damage/SFX. CI and local runtime checks now block the old cast-start exit pattern.
- Aatrox: the current mainline actor sheet was too uniformly red and muddy. The actor pass restored the requested earlier Darkin model with readable horns, black armor, giant sword, and larger attack/skill poses. The 2026-06-14 VFX follow-up replaces the active Q/W/R sheets from a fresh imagegen source, adds the Umbral Dash trail asset to `skill2`, and promotes World Ender from a small behind-actor aura to a visible demon-wing foreground buff.
- Viktor: the previous mainline/dirty VFX were too line-based. This pass replaces the tiny attack projectile and rebuilds laser, laser aftershock, shield, Gravity Field, Chaos Storm, storm impact, and evolution aura sheets with fuller image-generated Hextech machinery and energy. The rebuilt actor was enlarged inside its `57x54` frame cells and given small attack/skill/run silhouette accents so actions read as active model animation instead of static idle frames. The 2026-06-14 follow-up replaces Q/Siphon, E Death Ray, W Gravity Field, and R Chaos Storm again from local imagegen sources. E now uses wide magenta-cyan ground laser frames plus a separate aftershock burn instead of a thin line. W now spawns a high-z generated magnetic field directly on the target ground with a 0-delay long pulse. R now spawns high-z impact and storm ViewEffects directly at the target ground, uses a direct `ApplyInProjectile` opening hit, and keeps a long-lived generated storm pulse instead of hiding the visible storm behind a `ParabolicProjectile` anchor.
- Viktor visibility follow-up: Live evidence still showed sound-only or barely visible Q/E/W/R feedback despite the generated sheets being populated. The follow-up keeps those bitmap VFX assets and fixes their runtime data: Siphon Power's projectile now stays on screen for 18 ticks instead of disappearing after 6, Death Ray and aftershock render at foreground z and the main lane appears with 0 delay, Gravity Field is promoted to z=7, and Chaos Storm's impact/storm rows are promoted to z=8 with the persistent storm pulse starting immediately.
- Jhin: the old implementation still used placeholder projectile behavior and did not sell the LoL virtuoso identity. This pass preserves Jhin's native action-count contract while replacing the actor with a compact image-generated masked marksman whose legs, feet, and gun stay inside card/list/HUD bounds. Whisper, fourth shot, Dancing Grenade, Deadly Flourish, Captive Audience, and Curtain Call now reference generated red-gold lotus, bullet, and theater-stage runtime sheets; the old base-arrow projectile token is blocked by CI.
- Yasuo: the previous Q looked like a small curved wind slash instead of Steel Tempest's forward sword thrust, W read as a ground ring instead of Wind Wall, and R looked like a generic cyclone without airborne suspension. The 2026-06-14 rebuild uses local imagegen contact sheets chroma-keyed into the active runtime sheets: Q now keeps a visible katana blade and hilt through the thrust, Q3 is a compact vertical tornado, W rises from the ground as a vertical barrier with base dust, and R uses dark airborne silhouettes plus cyan-white sword arcs and a final drop impact. CI now has per-sheet gates for those silhouettes so the assets cannot regress to code-line wind placeholders.
- Audio: `sound/sfx` contains matching `.sound_info` and `.wav` clips for Aatrox, Darius, Thresh, Kayn, and Viktor attack casts/hits, skill casts/hits, and ult/skill voice lines. Champion data references were checked for the relevant attack, skill, skill2, and ult actions.
